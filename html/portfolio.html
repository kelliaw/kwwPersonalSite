Curriculum Designer (2020)

Hired by Jefferson County Public Schools, this curriculum was targeted for middle school grades (6-8) to bridge and spread cultural and racial awareness for students.

Kentucky state standards and Social Justice standards were utilized to create and build lesson objectives and assessments.

Project time was one month (40 hours per week for three weeks) to design a ten-lesson curriculum. This is designed to be implemented over six weeks.

I used different forms of digital media to make the content engaging for diverse learners. The formatting of the curriculum is flexible and can be modified in its entirety for virtual or in-person options.

Throughout the design process, I collaborated with fellow colleagues for design feedback and input.

Curriculum Design (2020-2021)

This was a website designed to organize and collect all resources and media for the virtual 2020-2021 school year. This curriculum is targeted for 6th grade learners to strengthen language arts and math skills (due to interrupted learning from COVID), spread awareness of digital literacy, focus on how to best utilize digital tools during virtual learning, how to protect your identity while using social media and digital platforms, and leadership skills.

All instruction and assessments were based on the Kentucky State Standards. Multiple forms of progress data from assessments were analyzed to determine the specific course of learning experiences.

At the most recent update, this project has lasted for over 25 weeks, implemented five days per week, five classes per day. Each class has 25-30 students with varying levels of proficiency. I use multiple forms of digital media to make the content engaging and appealing for 10-12 year olds. The formatting of the curriculum is flexible and can be modified in its entirety for virtual or in-person options.

Throughout the on-going design process, I collaborated with three different committees to review assessment data and make curriculum adjustments.
